How to Use

  1. Enter n (trials), p (success probability 0-1), k (specific value).
  2. Click Calculate.
  3. View mean, variance, PMF, CDF, and chart.

Binomial distribution models the number of successes in n independent Bernoulli trials. Mean μ = np, variance σ² = np(1−p). Examples: coin flips (p=0.5), defect rates, survey responses. Normal approximation valid when np ≥ 5 and n(1−p) ≥ 5.
