Gregorian calendar rules:
A year is a leap year if:
1. Divisible by 4 — leap year (e.g., 2024)
2. EXCEPT if divisible by 100 — NOT a leap year (e.g., 1900)
3. UNLESS also divisible by 400 — leap year (e.g., 2000)

Why? Earth orbits the Sun in ~365.2425 days. Adding 1 day every 4 years over-corrects slightly, so century years skip the leap day — unless divisible by 400.

How to Use

  1. Enter a year to check if it is a leap year.
  2. Set a range and click Find leap years to list all leap years in that range.

Leap years keep our calendar aligned with Earth's orbit (365.2425 days). Without them, seasons would drift by ~24 days every century. The Gregorian calendar (1582) refined the Julian system by skipping 3 leap days every 400 years.
