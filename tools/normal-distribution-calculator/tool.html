Normal (Gaussian) distribution is the bell curve. Z = (X−-/σ standardizes values. Empirical rule: 68% within ±1σ, 95% within ±2σ, 99.7% within ±3σ. Common critical Z: 1.645 (90% CI), 1.96 (95% CI), 2.576 (99% CI). Used in: IQ scores, heights, measurement errors, hypothesis testing.
