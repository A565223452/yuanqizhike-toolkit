How to Use

  1. Enter real and imaginary parts of two complex numbers.
  2. Select operation (add, subtract, multiply, divide, etc.).
  3. For power, enter exponent n.
  4. See result in rectangular and polar forms.

Complex numbers: z = a + bi where i² = -1. Magnitude: |z| = √(a²+b²). Argument: θ = atan2(b,a). Polar form: r(cos θ + i·sin θ). Euler's formula: e^(iθ) = cos θ + i·sin θ. Used in engineering, physics, signal processing.
