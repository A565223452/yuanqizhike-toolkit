How to Use

  1. Switch direction: °F -°C or °C -°F.
  2. Enter temperature — instant conversion.
  3. Reference common oven temperatures below.

Formula: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9. °F = °C × 9/5 + 32. The Fahrenheit scale was created by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit in 1724. Water freezes at 32°F (0°C) and boils at 212°F (100°C). Most countries use Celsius; the US uses Fahrenheit.
