How to Use

  1. Enter your annual income and monthly debts.
  2. Set down payment and interest rate.
  3. Choose DTI rule: 28/36 (conservative, recommended), 35/45 (aggressive), or 43% (FHA max).
  4. See how much house you can afford.

The 28/36 rule: spend max 28% of gross monthly income on housing (PITI - Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance), and max 36% on all debts combined. Lenders use this to qualify borrowers. FHA loans allow up to 43%. Always leave buffer for maintenance (1% of home value annually) and emergencies.
